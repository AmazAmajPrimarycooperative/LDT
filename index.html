About Our Cooperative

Amaz Amaj Primary Cooperative PTY LTD is a community-driven agricultural enterprise built on trust, shared ownership, and sustainable farming.

Our Mission

To empower rural communities through cooperative agriculture, creating sustainable livelihoods by aggregating household garden plots into productive farming blocks — without displacing families from their ancestral land.

Our Vision

To become Africa's leading cooperative farming model — proving that community-owned agriculture can deliver commercial-scale returns while preserving land rights, feeding families, and creating dignified employment for the youth.

Our Land Model

The land is a standalone big garden — not one large commercial estate. Each household in our partner villages owns between 2 to 4 standard garden plots, depending on family size and village allocation.

Through signed MOUs, these household gardens are aggregated into a cooperative farming block — without displacing anyone. Families keep ownership of their plots while benefiting from shared machinery, inputs, and market access that only scale can provide.

How It Works

Each household contributes 2–4 garden plots to the cooperative block

Land ownership stays with the family — always

The cooperative provides seeds, fertilizer, machinery & market access

Revenue is shared proportionally based on land contributed

Our Journey

2019

Cooperative Founded

Amaz Amaj Primary Cooperative PTY LTD was established with a vision to transform rural agriculture.

2020

First MOU Signed

Initial agreements with partner villages to aggregate household garden plots into cooperative farming blocks.

2021

Hemp Cultivation Begins

800 hectares allocated for industrial hemp — fiber, seed, and CBD for premium markets.

2022

Livestock Program Launch

Introduced cattle, sheep, goats, and poultry to diversify revenue and create rapid cash flow.

2023

First Export Deal

Secured off-take agreements for hemp fiber and commercial grain for regional and export markets.

2024–2026

Scaling Operations

Expanding to 2,500+ hectares, 150+ partner households, and establishing value-added processing facilities.
